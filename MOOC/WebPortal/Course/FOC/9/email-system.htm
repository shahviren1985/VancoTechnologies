Electronic mail, most commonly referred to as email is a method of exchanging digital messages from an author to one or more recipients. Modern email operates across the Internet or other computer networks. Today's email systems are based on a store-and-forward model. Email servers accept, forward, deliver, and store messages. Neither the users nor their computers are required to be online simultaneously; they need connect only briefly, for as long as it takes to send or receive messages.

Any email message consists of two sections :

  1. Header – Structured into fields such as From, To, CC, Subject, Date, and other information about the email.
  2. Body – The basic content, as unstructured text; sometimes contains a signature block at the end. This is exactly the same as the body of a regular letter.
