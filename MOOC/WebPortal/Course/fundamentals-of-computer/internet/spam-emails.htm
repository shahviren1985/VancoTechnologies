Spam is any kind of email that you don’t want and that you didn’t sign up to receive. Some spam is annoying but harmless, but some might be part of an identity theft scam or other kind of fraud. You can get spam in instant messages, text messages, and on your social networking sites.

Best practices while dealing with SPAM emails

  • Add people you know to your safe sender list and unwanted senders to your blocked list
  • Report junk mail: If you get an email message that looks like spam or a phishing scam, report it.
  • Share your email address only with people you know
  • Ignore junk email
