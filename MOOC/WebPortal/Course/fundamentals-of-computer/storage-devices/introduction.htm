Most components in a computer can take one of two states: on/off; we can represent these states by the binary digits 0 (off) and 1 (on). Each binary digit (or bit) has two possible values (0, 1), but groups of binary digits can be used to represent larger numbers. Most computer systems use groups of 8 bits as their basic building blocks. These 8-bit groups are referred to as bytes.

Storage space is normally expressed in bytes. A kilobyte (K) is 1,024 bytes. A megabyte (MB) is 1,024 kilobytes. A gigabyte (GB) is 1,024 megabytes. A terabyte is 1,024 gigabytes. A megabyte is sometimes “rounded down” to only 1,000 kilobytes, and likewise for the larger units.

Computers are designed to allow data to be input, processed, stored, and output. In this chapter, we'll be taking a casual look at a computer's design for storing data.
