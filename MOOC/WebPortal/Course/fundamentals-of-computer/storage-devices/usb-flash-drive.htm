A USB flash drive is a data storage device that includes flash memory with an integrated Universal Serial Bus (USB) interface. USB flash drives are typically removable and rewritable, and physically much smaller than an optical disc. Most weigh less than 30 grams. USB flash drives use the USB mass storage standard, supported by most of the modern operating systems.

USB drives can store more data and transfer faster than much larger optical disc drives like CD-RW or DVD-RW drives mainly because USB Drives have no moving parts. USB flash drives are often used for the same purpose for storage, back-up and transfer of computer files.
